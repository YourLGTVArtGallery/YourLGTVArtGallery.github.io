Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

TV Art is an LG webOS app that displays your personal photos as a fullscreen ambient slideshow on your TV. This policy explains what data the app accesses, stores, and transmits.

What we collect

TV Art does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on any server. There is no backend. All data stays on your TV.

What is stored on your TV

The app stores the following in your TV's localStorage (local storage on the device, cleared when the app is uninstalled):

  • Authentication tokens — access token and refresh token from your storage provider (OneDrive). These are used to fetch your images and are never transmitted to any server other than the provider's own API.
  • Session data — which provider and folder or album you selected, so the slideshow resumes on next launch.
  • Settings — display preferences such as slideshow duration, image order, and active hours.
  • Hidden image list — IDs of images you've hidden from the slideshow.

Third-party services

Depending on the provider you connect, the app communicates with:

Microsoft OneDrive (Microsoft Graph API)
Used to authenticate your Microsoft account and list images from your OneDrive. Authentication uses the OAuth 2.0 Device Authorization Grant (RFC 8628) — you approve access on your phone or computer, not on the TV. Microsoft's privacy policy applies: privacy.microsoft.com.

Mylio Photos+
Used to fetch images from a shared Mylio album. No authentication is required — the app fetches a public manifest and images directly from Mylio's CDN using a share link you provide. Mylio's privacy policy applies: mylio.com/privacy-policy.

Google Drive
Used to fetch images from a publicly shared Google Drive folder. The app uses a Google API key to list files; no Google account login is required. Google's privacy policy applies: policies.google.com/privacy.

No analytics or tracking

TV Art contains no analytics, crash reporting, advertising SDKs, or third-party tracking of any kind.

Children's privacy

TV Art does not knowingly collect any information from children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If the policy changes materially, the updated date above will reflect the change. The current version is always at this URL.
